The Three Models

Model A — Current

10% total · No floor

5% from artist + 5% from venue. No minimum.
Deposit held20% nightly rate
Artist fee5% of nightly rate
Venue fee5% of nightly rate
Artist pays at conf.25% of nightly rate
Venue receives15% of nightly rate
GigNVenue earns10% of nightly rate
As % of deposit50% always
Minimum earningsNone
Model B — Proposed

5% total · $100 floor

2.5% from artist + 2.5% from venue. $100 minimum on GigNVenue total.
Deposit held20% nightly rate
Artist feemax($50, 2.5%)
Venue feemax($50, 2.5%)
Artist pays at conf.20% + max($50, 2.5%)
Venue receives20% − max($50, 2.5%)
GigNVenue earnsmax($100, 5% of nightly)
As % of deposit25% above floor
Minimum earnings$100 per show
⚠ Floor active below $2,000/night. Each party pays flat $50.
Model C — Alternative

7.5% total · $100 floor

5% from artist (unchanged) + 2.5% from venue. Artist cost stays same; venues get more.
Deposit held20% nightly rate
Artist fee5% of nightly rate
Venue feemax($50, 2.5%)
Artist pays at conf.25% (no change)
Venue receives20% − max($50, 2.5%)
GigNVenue earnsmax($100, 7.5% of nightly)
As % of deposit37.5% above floor
Minimum earnings$100 per show
⚠ Floor on venue fee only. Below $2,000: venue pays flat $50; artist fee unchanged at 5%.

Key Observations

Model A: Highest GigNVenue revenue, highest friction GigNVenue earns 10% of every successful show with no floor needed — strong revenue at scale. Downside: artist cost (25%) and lower venue release (15%) are the highest barriers to both sides of the marketplace. No protection below $1,000/night.
Model B: Best for both parties, weakest for GigNVenue Artists pay less, venues receive significantly more — strongest acquisition story on both sides. GigNVenue revenue is halved above the floor (5% vs 10%). The floor provides $100 minimum per show but applies to any booking below $2,000/night.
Model C: Middle path — venues win, GigNVenue earns more than B Artist cost is unchanged from today (25%), eliminating one variable. Venues get the same improvement as Model B (17.5%). GigNVenue earns 7.5% — 50% more than Model B while still being better for venues than the current model. Floor only affects the venue-side fee.
All three models agree at $1,000/night At exactly $1,000/night all three produce identical outcomes: artist pays $250, venue receives $150, GigNVenue earns $100. This is a useful anchor point when communicating the model change — at the median show price, nothing changes.
$2,000/night is the critical threshold for Model B Below $2,000, Model B's $100 floor is active and GigNVenue actually earns less than Model A at the same price point (floor vs. 10%). Above $2,000, GigNVenue earns half of Model A. Only the floor range (below $2,000) benefits GigNVenue relative to Model A.
Consider a minimum listed nightly rate of $500 Below $500/night, the $100 floor consumes the entire deposit in Models B and C, leaving the venue with nothing from escrow. A platform minimum listing price of $500/night is a clean way to avoid this edge case entirely.
